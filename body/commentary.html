§—II

  [A] The saying, “Can God create a rock he can’t lift,” is a frivolous attempt to undermine the all powerful nature of God. It is asking God to do something that defies all logic, that which he will not do. God brings himself—in our mortality—to exist in logic and reason. This is similar to if God could create a square circle.
  [B] A sequence or plurality of gods is not plausible for the same reason as point a. God acts in logic, and thus will not create something matching or exceeding his power because to commit such an act is illogical.
